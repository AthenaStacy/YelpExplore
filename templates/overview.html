Overview of Yelp review analysis


The Overall Project:

1) Used python and various python packages (pandas, scipy, scikit-learn...) to analyize several gigabytes of Yelp review data -- 2 million reviews from the past decade.

2) Looking into these questions:
What draws Yelpers to write return follow-up reviews?
What keeps them coming back to a business?
How can businesses use this to maintain good customer relations?

3) What I've found so far:
Restaurant reviews on the whole are positive and are mostly concerned with quality of food. Yelpers who write return reviews are wordier and are significantly more concerned with good service.

The text length of reviews decreases with star rating. Unhappy reviewers have more to say. First-time reviews, those destined to be followed up with a return review, are unusually long.

On the other hand there do not seem to be significant global time trends in star-ratings. Evolution of average Yelp ratings over the past decade have not fluctuated significantly, except for an unusually high rating in the first year. Month-to-month variations are minimal, implying seasons or time-of-year has negligible effect.

Both one-star and five-star reviews have the same most frequent bigram - "customer service." If Yelpers write return-reviewers, the subject is weighted more towards service than food.

The Takeways:

1) Yelp reviews are overall positive, and have stayed that over the seasons and years.

2) Infrequent/friendless Yelpers who are drawn to write their rare reviews rate more often at the extremes of one-star and five-stars.

3) Yelpers who review the same place more than once tend to be initially more verbose and more concerned with customer service. The follow-up reviews are even more focused on good or terrible service.

4) Yelpers love love love sweet potato fries.
